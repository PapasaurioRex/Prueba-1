Resistors

Resistors are electronic components that resist the flow of electric current. They are essential for controlling the amount of current flowing through a circuit.

What is a Resistor?

A resistor is a passive two-terminal electrical component that implements electrical resistance as a circuit element. Resistors oppose the flow of electric current.

Purpose of Resistors

Resistors are used in circuits to:

  • Limit current
  • Divide voltage
  • Create specific voltage drops
  • Set time constants in circuits

Units of Resistance

The unit of resistance is the ohm (Î©). One ohm is defined as the resistance of a conductor in which a current of one ampere is produced by an applied potential difference of one volt.

Resistor Color Code

The resistor color code is a system used to indicate the resistance value of a resistor. Each color represents a specific numerical value.

Types of Resistors

Common types of resistors include:

  • Carbon film resistors
  • Wire-wound resistors
  • Metal film resistors
  • Surface-mount resistors

Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law states that the voltage (V) across a resistor is directly proportional to the current (I) flowing through it, and the proportionality constant is the resistance (R). This can be represented by the formula: V = IR.

Examples of Resistors in Real-World Applications

Resistors are used in a wide variety of applications, including:

  • Electronic circuits
  • Sensors
  • Power supplies
  • Audio amplifiers
